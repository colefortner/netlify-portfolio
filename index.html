Things I enjoy outside of coding

Music

I love music of all kinds! Whether it is listening, playing or attending live shows, I am there for it. I have been to well over 100 live shows. If I am surfing the net for any real amount of time, I will inevitably end up at NPR Tiny Desk's YouTube channel.

Travel

One of my absolute favorite things is exploring new places domestic or abroad. You will be hard pressed to find a bigger fan of the unplanned road trip. New Zealand is my favorite place I have visited so far. The next trip I would like to take is a hiking tour through the towns leading up to Machu Picchu. I just want to sit up there in wonder, taking it all in amongst the alpacas.

Animals

If you have spent any time looking at my portfolio, you will notice that I am a lover of animals, especially dogs. Dogs are simply the best things on this planet. A goal of mine is to have a small hobby farm just outside a city where I can host a handful of rescue animals, and maybe grow some veggies. I would love to use my coding skills to help rescue organizations.

Reading

I really enjoy books that teach me something new about the world. My choices generally fall into one of three categories of interest... history, statistical analysis or gaining insight from a different perspective. So far this year I have read Nathaniel Philbrick's 'Mayflower', 'Narrative of the Captivity and Restoration of Mrs. Mary Rowlandson' by Mary Rowlandson, and I am currently reading 'The Last King of America' by Andrew Roberts. I often go down topic rabbit holes in search of a fuller context. My current topic is the origins of the US.
